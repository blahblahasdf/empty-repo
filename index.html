How-to Guide

Quick refreshers for the less-obvious movements.

Reference

Core

Bird dogs

Start on hands and knees. Extend opposite arm and leg, pause, then return slowly. Imagine a glass of water on your lower back and do not twist your hips.

Side plank

Forearm on floor, elbow under shoulder, lift hips and hold. Bend knees to make it easier. Stop before form collapses.

Prone cobra hold

Lie face-down, arms at sides or slightly out. Gently lift chest a little and squeeze shoulder blades down and back. Keep neck long.

Curl-ups

Lie on your back, knees bent. Curl shoulder blades slightly off the floor, pause, then lower slowly. Small and controlled beats full sit-ups.

Push and shoulders

Shoulder taps / pike push-ups

Shoulder taps: alternate tapping opposite shoulders from push-up position. Pike push-ups: hips high, lower head toward floor, push back up.

Legs and glutes

Reverse lunges

Step one foot backward, lower back knee toward floor, push through front foot to stand. Keep feet on railroad tracks, not a tightrope.

Glute bridges

Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat. Squeeze glutes and lift hips until shoulders-to-knees form a line.

Slow mountain climbers

From push-up position, bring one knee toward chest, return it, then switch. 20 total means 10 each leg.

Jug exercises

One-arm jug rows

Support one hand on a chair/table/workbench. Hinge forward and pull the jug toward your hip. Lower slowly.

Hammer curls

Hold jug by the handle with thumb/handle side up. Curl smoothly, lower slowly, and avoid swinging.

Suitcase carry

Hold jug at one side and walk or stand tall. Do not lean away from the jug. Brace lightly.

General rule: muscle effort is fine. Sharp pain, joint pain, dizziness, chest pain, or unusual shortness of breath means stop and adjust.

Progression rule: each week, improve only one thing—more reps, one added set, one harder bike block, or slightly higher output. Do not upgrade everything at once.
