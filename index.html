Exercise How-to Guide

Tap an exercise for cues, easier versions, and common mistakes.

Reference
Incline / knee / regular push-ups

Choose the version that lets you do clean reps without bad-foot pain. Incline push-ups are the preferred foot-friendly option if you have a sturdy counter, workbench, or table.

  • Incline: hands on a sturdy raised surface, body straight, lower chest toward surface.
  • Knee: knees on mat/towel, body straight from shoulders to knees.
  • Regular: full push-up position only if your foot feels okay.
  • Do not add tons of extra reps to “make up” for an easier version. Controlled reps win.
Reverse lunges

Stand tall, step one foot backward, lower your back knee toward the floor, then push through the front foot to stand. Alternate legs.

  • Chest tall.
  • Front knee tracks over toes.
  • Use a chair or wall for balance.
  • If the bad foot hurts, switch to chair squats.
Chair squats

Stand in front of a chair, bench, or sturdy box. Sit your hips back, lightly touch the chair with your butt, then stand back up.

  • Keep your whole foot planted.
  • Knees track roughly over toes.
  • Sit back like you’re closing a car door with your butt.
  • Do not bounce off the chair.
Pike push-ups / shoulder taps

Pike push-up: hips high in an upside-down V, lower head toward floor, push back up. Shoulder tap: push-up position, alternate tapping opposite shoulders.

  • Use shoulder taps first if pike push-ups feel awkward.
  • Keep hips from rocking during shoulder taps.
  • Elevate hands if the floor position bothers your foot.
Glute bridges

Lie on your back, knees bent, feet flat. Squeeze glutes and lift hips until your body forms a line from shoulders to knees.

  • Push through heels.
  • Squeeze at the top.
  • Do not aggressively arch your lower back.
Dead bugs

Lie on your back, arms up, knees bent at 90 degrees. Slowly lower opposite arm and leg while keeping lower back gently pressed toward the floor.

  • Move slowly.
  • Exhale as you extend.
  • Stop before your back arches.
Prone Y-T-W raises

Lie face-down and lift your arms slightly in Y, T, and W shapes. Tiny controlled movements are enough.

  • Keep neck relaxed.
  • Think shoulder blades down and back.
  • Do not shrug toward your ears.
Side plank

Forearm on the floor, elbow under shoulder, lift hips and hold. Bend knees to make it easier.

  • Keep body in a straight line.
  • Do not let hips rotate forward or backward.
Prone cobra hold

Lie face-down. Put arms at your sides or slightly out from your body. Gently lift your chest a little, squeeze shoulder blades down and back, and hold.

  • Keep your neck long; do not crank your head up.
  • This should feel like upper-back/posture work.
  • If it bothers your low back, make the lift smaller.
  • This replaces reverse plank for now.
Slow mountain climbers

Start in push-up position. Bring one knee toward chest, return it, then switch. Slow and controlled, not frantic.

  • 20 total means 10 each leg.
  • Keep hips low.
  • Hands can be elevated on a couch/counter to make it easier.
Garage setup

You do not need a gym setup, but a small clear floor area will make this less janky.

  • Use a yoga mat, folded towel, or blanket.
  • Wear shoes for standing moves if that helps the foot.
  • Use a sturdy chair, workbench, or counter for incline push-ups and balance.
  • Clear enough space to lie down and extend your arms.
Pain rule: muscle effort is fine. Sharp pain, joint pain, dizziness, chest pain, unusual shortness of breath, or meaningful bad-foot pain means stop and adjust.

Progression rule: each week, improve only one thing—more reps, one added set, one harder bike block, or slightly higher output. Do not upgrade everything at once.
